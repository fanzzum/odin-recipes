HOW TO MAKE OMURICE




Description

Omurice is a Japanese comfort food combining "omelet" and "rice." It features fried rice,
often flavored with ketchup and mixed with vegetables or meat, wrapped in a soft, fluffy
omelet. Typically garnished with a drizzle of ketchup, this dish is both savory and slightly
sweet, offering a delightful blend of textures and flavors.



Ingredients

  • Cooked rice - 2 cups
  • Chicken (diced) or any protein - 150 g
  • Onion (finely chopped) - 1 small
  • Carrot (finely chopped) - 1 small
  • Green peas or corn - 1/4 cup (optional)
  • Ketchup - 3 tablespoons
  • Soy sauce - 1 teaspoon
  • Salt and pepper - to taste
  • Eggs - 4 (2 per omelet)
  • Milk or cream (optional, for fluffy eggs) - 2 tablespoons
  • Butter or oil - 2 tablespoons
  • Ketchup (for garnish) - as needed


STEPS

1. **Prepare the Fried Rice**
Heat a tablespoon of oil or butter in a pan over medium heat. Saut√© one small finely
chopped onion, a finely chopped small carrot, and any other vegetables like green peas
or corn until softened. Add 150 grams of diced chicken (or your choice of protein) and cook
it thoroughly. Stir in two cups of cooked rice, three tablespoons of ketchup, a teaspoon of
soy sauce, and season with salt and pepper. Stir everything well and set the fried rice aside.
2. **Make the Omelet**
In a bowl, whisk together two eggs, a tablespoon of milk or cream (optional), and a pinch of
salt. Heat a tablespoon of oil or butter in a non-stick pan over medium-low heat. Pour the egg
mixture into the pan and swirl it to cover the bottom evenly. Cook gently, ensuring the omelet
stays soft and slightly runny on top.
3. **Assemble the Omurice**
Once the omelet is nearly set, place half of the fried rice on one side of the omelet. Fold the
omelet over the rice, creating a half-moon shape.
4. **Serve and Garnish**
Slide the omelet carefully onto a plate with the seam side down. Drizzle ketchup on top and serve immediately.
