HOW TO MAKE A FRENCH TOAST




Description

French toast is a delicious breakfast dish made from sliced bread
soaked in a mixture of eggs, milk, and spices, then fried until golden
brown. It's often served with syrup, powdered sugar, fruits or whipped
cream. Its crispy outside contrasts perfectly with a soft, fluffy interior.



Ingredients

  • Eggs (2-3 large eggs)
  • Milk (1/2 cup)
  • Sugar (1-2 tablespoons)
  • Vanilla extract (1 teaspoon)
  • Cinnamon powder (1/2 teaspoon)
  • Salt (a pinch)
  • Bread slices (4-6, preferably slightly stale)
  • Butter (for greasing the pan)
  • Optional toppings:
    - Syrup (e.g., maple syrup)
    - Powdered sugar
    - Fresh fruits (e.g., berries, bananas)
    - Nuts (e.g., almonds, walnuts)
    - Whipped cream


STEPS

1. **Prepare the Batter**
Crack the eggs into a large mixing bowl and whisk until smooth. Add the milk, sugar, vanilla
extract, cinnamon powder, and a pinch of salt. Whisk until everything is well combined.
2. **Heat the Pan**
Place a non-stick skillet or griddle over medium heat and let it warm up. Add a
small pat of butter to the pan and let it melt, spreading it evenly across the surface.
3. **Dip the Bread**
Take one slice of bread and dip it into the batter. Ensure both sides
are coated but avoid soaking it for too long, as this can make the bread soggy.
4. **Cook the French Toast**
Place the battered bread slice onto the hot skillet. Cook for 2-3 minutes on each side, or until the bread is golden
brown and slightly crisp on the edges. Repeat with the remaining slices, adding more butter to the pan as needed.
5. **Keep Warm**
If making a large batch, keep the cooked French toast slices warm
by placing them on a baking sheet in an oven set to 200°F (90°C).
6. **Serve**
Stack the French toast slices on a plate. Add your favorite toppings: drizzle
syrup, sprinkle powdered sugar, or top with fresh fruits, nuts, or whipped cream.
